Jessie's Piano Studio offers piano lessons for students of all ages and abilities.

Everyone has the potential to develop an outlet in music, and playing piano can help establish this foundation of love for music. It's truly one of the best, lifelong gifts a parent can give to their child. Jessie recommends beginning piano lessons at about the age of 5. Students are able to choose from a wide variety of music styles. Piano recitals are optional, low-pressure, and offered throughout the year for all students. Whatever your age, it will be an unforgettable experience.

Jessie offers private lessons to ensure each student learns in the manner that is best suited for them. She is committed to offering a great learning experience. Each private lesson is 30 minutes long with the learning material based upon the desires and needs of the student.

Benefits of Learning and Playing Music

Pete Linforth from Pixabay

Cognitive Benefits

Researchers found that those who played an instrument for two years showed a stronger "neurophysiological distinction" between certain sounds than children who didn't get the instrumental training. For instance, the music-makers more easily could tell the difference between the words "bill" and "pill," a key skill in learning to read (Skoe, E. & Kraus, N. (2012). A Little Goes a Long Way: How the Adult Brain Is Shaped by Musical Training in Childhood, Journal of Neuroscience, 32, 34, 11510. DOI: 10.1523/JNEUROSCI.1949-12.2012).

Educational Benefits

The College Entrance Examination Board found that students involved in public school music programs scored 107 points higher on the SAT's than students with no participation (Profiles of SAT and Achievement Test Takers, The College Board, compiled by the Music Educators National Conference, 2002).

Health Benefits

Playing the piano offers different physical and physiological advantages. Regular piano playing sharpens fine motor skills and improves eye-hand coordination in children. It can also reduce anxiety, heart and respiratory rates, and lower blood pressure. Research has shown that piano lessons for adults can have a significant impact on increased levels of Human Growth Hormone, which slows the adverse effects of aging.
